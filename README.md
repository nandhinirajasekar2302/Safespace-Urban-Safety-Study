# SafeSpace: Advancing Inclusive Urban Security Through Innovation 

## Overview 
This academic study investigates public perceptions of women’s and LGBTQ+ safety in urban environments through a structured survey-based study. 

## Methodology 
	• Quantitative survey design (Google Forms) 
	• Hypothesis formulation and supporting literature review 
	• Descriptive statistical analysis of responses 
	• Policy-oriented interpretation of findings  

## Focus Areas 
	• Gender norms and safety perception 
	• Public attitudes toward AI-enabled surveillance and smart infrastructure 
	• Socio-technical system design for urban safety 

## Key Outcome 
Formulated research-based recommendations integrating technological, institutional, and social dimensions of urban safety. 

## Academic Context 
This project was developed as part of undergraduate coursework to explore socio-technical approaches to urban governance. The proposed interventions are conceptual and intended for research discussion.
